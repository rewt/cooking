## Ingredients

### Meat & dairy
- 1 lb 80/20 ground chuck
- 1 lb 90/10 ground sirloin (or lean ground beef 90/10)
- 1 lb fresh Mexican chorizo (raw)
- 1.5 Tbsp butter

### Produce (prep amounts & cuts)
- Yellow onion (1 large), finely chopped → ~2 cups
- Jalapeño (1), small dice → ~¼ cup (seeds in = hotter)
- Serrano peppers (3), small dice → ~3 Tbsp
- Red bell pepper (1½ total), medium dice → ~2 cups
- Celery (2 stalks), fine dice → ~¾ cup
- Garlic (4–5 cloves), minced → ~1½–2 Tbsp
- Limes (2–3)

### Dried chiles (smoky base + bright heat)
- 3 ancho (on hand)
- 2 New Mexico (on hand)
- 1 pasilla negro (on hand)
- 1 chipotle meco (on hand)
- 4 chiles de árbol (bright heat; hold back 1–3 for late crumble)

### Canned / jarred
- Sun-dried tomatoes (small can, drained if oil-packed)
- Crushed tomatoes (≈21 oz)
- Black beans (15 oz), drained & rinsed

### Spices & pantry
- Cumin, total 1.5 Tbsp (toast & grind if whole)
- ½ tsp smoked paprika (or 1 tsp sweet paprika for less smoke)
- Ground coriander 1 tsp
- Red pepper flakes 2 tsp
- Cayenne ¼–½ tsp (finish, optional)
- Kosher salt & black pepper
- Worcestershire 1.5 Tbsp
- Apple cider vinegar 1 Tbsp
- Distilled white vinegar (purée + finish)
- Beef stock (soak/deglaze; have ≥2 cups)
- Masa harina (1–2 Tbsp, optional)
- ½–1 tsp sugar or tiny square dark chocolate (optional balance)
- (Optional) Tomato paste 1 tsp

---

## Method (purée → aromatics → meats → combine → simmer partially covered → finish)

### A) Make the chile purée (BLENDER)
1. **Soak dried chiles** (ancho, New Mexico, pasilla, meco, and árbol) in hot stock or hot water until pliable.
2. **Strain & reserve soaking liquid; keep ¾ cup total**:
   - ½ cup for purée
   - ¼ cup for deglaze
3. Into blender now:
   - All soaked chiles (ancho, New Mexico, pasilla, meco, **plus 3 árbol**)
   - *(Hold back 1–3 árbol for the finish.)*
4. Add:
   - ½ cup strained soak liquid
   - Sun-dried tomatoes
   - 1–2 tsp white vinegar
   - ½–1 tsp sugar
   - (Optional) 1 tsp tomato paste
5. Blend very smooth (60–90 sec).
   - If thick, splash in 1–2 Tbsp more liquid.
   - (Optional: strain for ultra-smooth.)
6. Set aside.

---

### B) Sauté aromatics (MAIN POT)
1. Pot on medium-high; add **1.5 Tbsp butter**.
2. Add onion, jalapeño, serranos, bell pepper, celery; sauté **5–7 min** to translucent edges.
3. Add garlic halfway; cook **30–60 sec** until fragrant.
4. Transfer aromatics to bowl.
5. Leave **1–2 Tbsp fat** in pot; keep heat on.

---

### C) Brown the meats in batches (MAIN POT)
> Brown in the empty pot, **not** atop the veg.

1. **Chorizo** (1 lb, casing off): brown to crisp edges, **6–8 min**. Transfer to bowl.
   - Spoon off excess fat, leave ~**1–2 Tbsp**.
2. **Chuck** (1 lb) in 2–3 batches:
   - Thin layer, light pinch salt
   - Don’t stir **2–3 min** to crust
   - Break and brown **2–3 min** more
   - Transfer to bowl.
3. **Sirloin** (1 lb): same method, slightly faster.
   - If fat > **3–4 Tbsp**, spoon off; aim to keep **1–2 Tbsp**.

---

### D) Deglaze, reunite & season (MAIN POT)
1. Deglaze with **¼ cup reserved soak liquid** (or stock), scraping fond.
2. Reduce to syrupy (**30–60 sec**).
3. Return meats + aromatics (and juices).
4. Stir in:
   - Chile purée
   - Crushed tomatoes
   - Worcestershire (**1.5 Tbsp**)
   - Apple cider vinegar (**1 Tbsp**)

**Spice Set A (add now = ⅔ of each):**
- Cumin **1 Tbsp**
- Ground coriander **⅔ tsp**
- Paprika: **~⅓ tsp smoked** (or **⅔ tsp sweet**)
- Red pepper flakes **~1⅓ tsp**
- Salt & black pepper to taste

If too thick, add a splash of stock/soak.

---

### E) Simmer (partially covered) & finish (MAIN POT)
1. Bring to a gentle simmer.
2. Lid partially covered (ajar) and hold a low simmer **2–4 hours**, stirring every **20–30 min**.

**Adjusting thickness**
- Thicker needed? Slide lid off (uncovered) for the last **30–60 min**.
- Reducing too fast? Cover a bit more and lower heat; add a splash of stock.

**Beans**
- Stir in black beans during the last **30–45 min**.

**Bright-heat finish (last 10–15 min)**
1. Crumble reserved árbol (**1–3 pods**) into pot.
2. Spice Set B (remaining ⅓):
   - Cumin **1½ tsp**
   - Coriander **⅓ tsp**
   - Paprika **~⅙ tsp smoked** (or **~⅓ tsp sweet**)
   - Red flakes **~⅔ tsp**
3. Optional cayenne **¼–½ tsp** for extra clean lift.
4. Acid to pop:
   - Add **1 tsp lime + 1 tsp white vinegar**
   - Taste; take total acid to **1–2 Tbsp** (lime + vinegar).
5. Balance (only if needed):
   - **½–1 tsp sugar** or a tiny square of **dark chocolate**
   - Simmer **2–3 min**.
6. Optional thickness:
   - Whisk **1–2 Tbsp masa harina** with water
   - Stir in; simmer **10 min**.

---

## Optional table sauce (serve on the side)

### Árbol–Lime “Bright Booster”
Blend:
- 3 toasted/soaked árbol
- 2 Tbsp lime juice
- 1 Tbsp white vinegar
- ¼ tsp salt
- ½ tsp sugar
- 2–3 Tbsp soaking liquid (or water)

Blend smooth. Stir 1–2 Tbsp into the pot if you want more sparkle; serve the rest at the table.
